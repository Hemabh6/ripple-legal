Ripple — Child Safety Standards

Last updated: 6 July 2026 · Applies to the Ripple app (package in.ripplesocial.app)

Ripple is committed to keeping its platform safe and has zero tolerance for child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE) in any form. This page describes our published standards against CSAE and how we prevent, detect, report, and act on it.

1. Prohibited content and behaviour

The following are strictly prohibited on Ripple and result in immediate account termination and, where applicable, reporting to authorities:

  • Child sexual abuse material (CSAM) of any kind — real, altered, drawn, or generated — in posts, stories, messages, profiles, or media
  • Grooming: contacting, befriending, or manipulating minors for sexual purposes
  • Sextortion, sexualisation of minors, or facilitating child trafficking or exploitation in any way

2. Age requirements

Ripple is not directed at children. Users must be at least 13 years old (or the higher minimum age required in their country) to create an account, as stated in our privacy policy.

3. In-app reporting

Every user can report any post or account directly in the app: tap the ⋯ menu on a post → Report post, or open a profile and choose Report user. A dedicated "Child safety concern" category ensures such reports are prioritised. Users can also block any account instantly.

4. Review and enforcement

  • Child-safety reports are treated with the highest priority and reviewed by the operator as quickly as possible
  • Confirmed CSAE content is removed immediately and the account is permanently terminated
  • Evidence is preserved in accordance with applicable law so it can be provided to law enforcement

5. Reporting to authorities

We comply with all applicable child safety laws, including the Indian Protection of Children from Sexual Offences (POCSO) Act, 2012 and the Information Technology Act, 2000. Confirmed CSAM is reported to the relevant regional and national authorities (in India, the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal at cybercrime.gov.in; internationally, the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children — NCMEC), and we cooperate fully with lawful investigations.
